    OWASP-guided Testing in Real-world Environments

    During internship, I performed security assessments against web systems in different business domains. The process emphasized repeatability, evidence collection, and direct remediation collaboration.

    Execution Flow

    1. Reconnaissance: endpoint mapping, parameter discovery, and attack surface prioritization.
    2. Validation: confirm exploitability with low-impact PoCs.
    3. Impact analysis: evaluate privilege boundary and business impact.
    4. Reporting: document CVSS score, exploit path, and patch guidance.
    5. Retest: verify fix quality and regression risk.

    Common Findings

    • Stored XSS through weak output encoding.
    • IDOR caused by missing object-level authorization checks.
    • Access control bypass due to inconsistent role validation.
    • Business logic issues in workflow state transitions.

    Reporting Template (CVSS-driven)

    Section Content Goal
    Summary Issue type, scope, affected assets Fast triage by stakeholders
    PoC Step-by-step reproduction Developer verification
    Impact CVSS score and business impact Prioritization for fixes
    Remediation Code and config recommendations Safe and complete patching
    A good report is not only about finding vulnerabilities. It must help engineering teams fix the issue correctly and quickly.
